Choosing the Right Countertop for Your Home: Vitrified vs Quartz vs Granite
General • 6 min read
When it comes to designing your dream home, every detail matters. One of the most significant aspects of your home interiors is the choice of countertops. Countertops not only serve functional purposes but also contribute to the overall aesthetic appeal of your space. With various options available, such as vitrified, Quartz, and Granite, it’s essential to understand the differences between them to make an informed decision that suits your lifestyle and preferences.
Vitrified Countertops: An Affordable Triumph in Durability
Crafted from a blend of clay, sand, and resins, vitrified countertops boast a unique fusion of durability and affordability. Fired at high temperatures, these surfaces emerge unscathed by stains and scratches. However, it’s important to note that while vitrified tiles can be made of both low-tech and high-tech materials, they often feature relatively thin and brittle surfaces. This means that they might not withstand the impact of heavy objects, accidental drops, or other potential hazards in the kitchen. The slabs are available in sizes of 12mm and 15mm thickness
Despite this consideration, vitrified countertops remain a cost-effective choice, particularly appealing to budget-conscious homeowners looking to revamp their living spaces. Vitrified countertops are available in a wide range of colours and patterns, making them a versatile choice for various interior styles. 
Quartz Countertops: Where Durability Meets Elegance
Quartz is an engineered stone made up of about 90% natural quartz crystals, combined with resins, pigments, and other materials for exceptional strength and durability. Its non-porous surface makes quartz countertops highly stain-resistant and easy to maintain, requiring only regular cleaning with mild soap and water.. While they may come with a slightly higher price tag, the blend of aesthetics and endurance is hard to beat. The slabs are available in sizes of 15mm and 20mm thickness
Quartz offers an extensive range of colors, patterns, and textures, imitating natural stones, solid hues, and even concrete. This variety ensures that interior designers can easily find a perfect match for any home décor. While its captivating appearance makes it a top choice for kitchen countertops, it’s important to note that quartz lacks high heat resistance. Therefore, avoid exposing quartz countertops to direct extreme heat.
Granite Countertops: The Timeless Beauty of Natural Stone
Granite countertops are a classic choice known for their natural beauty and durability. Each slab of granite is unique, boasting intricate patterns and a rich color palette. While granite countertops are heat and scratch-resistant, they do require periodic sealing to maintain their longevity. The slabs are available in sizes of 18mm and 25mm thickness
The natural allure of granite adds a touch of elegance to any kitchen or bathroom, making it a favorite among homeowners seeking a timeless appeal. They are relatively heavy compared to marble or quartz countertop material. So sometimes you may need to provide extra support.
Choosing Your Perfect Fit
So, which countertop is your perfect match? Well, it’s all about aligning your needs with your budget. Vitrified countertops excel in affordability and endurance. If you’re looking for an elegant yet low-maintenance option, quartz is a strong contender. Meanwhile, granite adds a touch of nature’s grandeur to your space.

While the above 3 type’s are the most widely used kitchen counter top material, few other counter tops do also make their mark like
Laminate Counter top: 
Laminate countertops are a popular choice for kitchens and bathrooms due to their affordability and versatility. They come in a wide range of colors, patterns, and textures, mimicking the look of natural stone, wood, or even metal. The slabs are available in sizes of 3mm, 6mm and 12mm thickness
These countertops are easy to clean and maintain, making them a practical option for busy households. Additionally, laminate countertops are resistant to stains and moisture, providing a durable and stylish surface for your home.
Butcher block or Wooden Counter top
Wooden countertops instantly infuse your kitchen with character and authenticity through their natural appearance. With proper care and maintenance, wooden countertops can last for years, adding a cozy and elegant touch to your home. Whether you prefer a rustic look or a more polished finish, wooden countertops offer a versatile option that complements a wide range of design styles. but yes, it needs maintenance! 
The slabs are available in sizes of 20mm, 30mm and 40mm thickness
From the deep, rich tones of mahogany to the lighter shades of maple or oak, each type of wood offers unique grain patterns and colors, ensuring that every wooden countertop is truly one-of-a-kind.
Concrete Counter Top
Concrete countertops offer a unique blend of durability and aesthetic appeal, making them a great choice for modern kitchens. Known for their hidden strength, 
these countertops can be customized with various colors, textures, and finishes to suit any design style. They provide a robust and stylish surface that can withstand the demands of a busy kitchen while adding a contemporary touch to your home.
Porcelain countertop
These high-quality slabs are created through the wet grinding of clayish raw materials, granite, metamorphic rocks containing feldspar, and ceramic pigments. They are then subjected to a hybrid firing process at a high temperature of 1200°C. This process results in a dense, non-porous material that is highly resistant to water and bacteria.
The slabs are available in sizes of 3mm and 12mm thickness, with dimensions of 3m in height and 1m in width for cladding surfaces. For kitchen applications, the slabs are offered in larger sizes, measuring 3.2m in height and 1.6m in width. so naturally they are Light in weight & offer high durability. But they are expensive
Soapstone Counter top
Soapstone countertops offer a unique and elegant option for kitchen surfaces, renowned for their smooth, matte texture and distinctive veining. Composed primarily is a metamorphic rock composed of 60%-70% magnesium-rich mineral talc, this dense and relatively non-porous natural stone resists stains and bacteria effectively. Interior or even Architectural-grade soapstone, used in countertops, contains 30%-50% talc and is quarried worldwide including in South East Asia and south America. This unique composition gives soapstone countertops a soft, dry, soap-like texture. Predominantly grey in color, these countertops feature veins in various shades such as black, blue, brown, white, and green, creating a distinctive and attractive look.
One of the most charming features of a white soapstone countertop is its ability to develop a natural patina over time, enhancing its beauty and adding to its timeless appeal. Soapstone can cost you anywhere between Rs.4000 & Rs.6000 per square foot & is available is 20mm thickness
Stainless Steel Counter top
Stainless steel countertops bring a sleek, modern look to kitchens while offering a durable and hygienic surface for cooking and food preparation. Made from a solid sheet of stainless steel, these countertops are resistant to heat, stains, and bacteria, making them a popular choice for both professional and residential kitchens. 
Their non-porous surface is easy to clean with just soap and water, making them ideal for busy cooks. SS sheets are pasted on a solid surface. while they are stain free but can involve a lot of maintenance to your already busy lives! SS sheets are expensive to work and fabricate, Rates may vary from 1000-1900/sq ft

Expect Lifelong Beauty: In the durability department, granite takes the lead, followed closely by quartz and vitrified surfaces.

Core Materials
Infographics • 5 min read
One the most ESSENTIAL core product in the wood manufacturing industry in India are PLYWOOD & HDHMR boards. Particle Boards are generally used in large scale bulk manufacturing industries. So, lets find about more about it!
Plywood
Plywood is a widely used engineered wood product, created by gluing together thin layers of wood veneers. Each ply is bonded with its grain running at right angles to the adjacent layers, providing exceptional strength and stability.
What is HDHMR Board?
HDHMR, or High-Density High Moisture Resistant board, is a type of engineered wood. It’s created by compressing hardwood fibers with a special adhesive under high pressure, resulting in a dense, durable, and moisture-resistant board. This makes HDHMR perfect for applications that require robustness and resistance to moisture, such as in kitchens, bathrooms, and other high-moisture environments.
What is Particle Board?
Particle board is a type of manufactured wood product created by combining wood chips, sawmill shavings, or sawdust with a synthetic resin or another adhesive, and then subjecting them to compression. While it is lightweight and affordable, it lacks the strength and durability of plywood or HDHMR. Its lower density makes it susceptible to damage from moisture and heavy objects.
STRENGTH
The Strength of Plywood compared to HDHMR (High-Density High Moisture Resistant board) and particle board is typically measured by evaluating their structural integrity, load-bearing capacity, and resistance to stress. Here are some key points:
Structural Integrity: Plywood is made from thin layers of wood veneer glued together with alternating grain directions, which enhances its strength and stability. HDHMR is made from compressed wood fibres, making it denser and more moisture resistant. Particle board is made from compressed wood chips, making it less strong and more prone to damage.
Load-Bearing Capacity: Plywood is known for its superior load-bearing capacity and is often used in construction for flooring, roofing, and walls. HDHMR also offers high strength and is suitable for areas exposed to moisture, such as kitchens and bathrooms. Particle board, on the other hand, is not suitable for weight-bearing applications as it tends to sag or split under heavy weight.
Resistance to Stress: Plywood’s cross-grain construction provides better resistance to warping, cracking, and impact. HDHMR is highly resistant to moisture and humidity, maintaining its structural integrity even in demanding environments. Particle board is more prone to damage when subjected to stress and is not moisture resistant.
In summary, plywood is generally stronger and more durable than both HDHMR and particle board, making it the preferred choice for cabinetry requiring structural strength
Cost
HDHMR Board: Generally, more expensive than standard plywood because of its advanced manufacturing process and enhanced properties like higher density and moisture resistance. 
Plywood: Offers a more budget-friendly option with adequate performance for many applications, making it a versatile and economical choice for various projects.
Application
Cabinetry
HDHMR: Perfect for kitchen and bathroom Shutters where surface evenness or moisture resistance is crucial.
Plywood: Commonly used for cabinet carcass but may need additional treatment (BWP Grade) in moisture-prone areas.
Wall/Ceiling Panels
HDHMR: Provides a high-quality finish for decorative panels and partitions which is finished using pigmentation processes such as Paint/Deco/Pu
Plywood: Versatile for various interior solutions, including wall paneling and false ceilings where laminate/Veneer is used for surface finishing
Installation and Workability
HDHMR Sheet vs. Plywood: HDHMR board is heavier and denser, requiring specialized tools for cutting and shaping. Plywood is easier to cut and shape with standard woodworking tools.

10. Does plywood bend?
Yes! Plywood may bend, usually because of one of two reasons..
Moisture difference between the two faces of the plywood panel. This is a very common occurrence. While stacking up plywood panels. the uppermost panel’s upper face is left exposed, while its other face side is unexposed in the stack. If the moisture content of the surrounding is higher than the plywood moisture content, the exposed surface tends to absorb moisture which in turn leads to expansion of that surface. All this time, the other surface facing down is not exposed and therefore does not absorb moisture. So the asymmetric expansion of the upper surface without any supporting expansion on the other side leads to bending of the ply.
Sticking laminates only to one side of the ply. So, Plywood is basically manufactured by pressing together an odd number of layers of wooden core veneers which are placed at right angles every alternate layer to structurally reinforce the long grains of the wooden core in a kind of wire mesh structure format. Now theses layers are always kept odd to maintain balance of the ply as both the outer side of the ply is made with long grain veneers. Now sticking a laminate only to one side of the ply disrupts this balance and puts more weight on one side than the other which ultimately leads to the ply bowing in shape. Can be easily rectified by sticking a plain sheet of laminate on the other side as well to balance out the weight.

Crafting Dream Spaces: Exploring Laminates and Acrylics for a Stylish Home
General • 3 min read
As a first-time homeowner in India, embarking on the journey of creating your dream living space can be both exhilarating and overwhelming. A vital aspect of this journey is selecting the perfect materials for your interiors. Enter laminates and acrylics – two distinct choices celebrated for their versatility and aesthetic appeal. While laminates and acrylics boast a variety of applications, it’s essential to note their specific uses. 
Laminates, available in thicknesses ranging from as slim as 0.4mm to a robust 15mm, boast varied applications: from wrapping around ply, MDF, or particle board surfaces to becoming your kitchen countertop or breakfast counters. It’s crucial to note, however, that only specific laminates, like the heat-resistant 15mm ones, are recommended for countertops. Meanwhile, acrylics, often seen as an alternative to the glass finish, are preferred for vertical surfaces due to their lightweight nature. This makes acrylic hardware more durable than its glass counterpart. However, acrylics are not heat-resistant, rendering them unsuitable for countertops.

Crafting Laminates: A Symphony of Layers

Laminate sheets are created by bonding together two or more layers of materials. Typically, laminate sheets made from paper are glued to wood to enhance the surface aesthetics.

Decorative Layer: Above the Kraft paper, a tissue paper impregnated with melamine formaldehyde (MF) resin offers protection and enhances abrasion resistance.

Core Sheet: The core layer consists of Kraft paper treated with phenol formaldehyde (PF).

Harmonising Hues: Unlocking the Power of Colour Combinations in Home Interiors
General • 2 min read
Colours are not just decorative elements; they are potent tools that can dramatically transform your living spaces. A well-thought-out colour palette can alter the mood of a room, influence your emotions, and even impact your well-being. So, let’s embark on an inspiring journey through the rich world of colour combinations, harmonising hues, and their potent effects on home interiors.
The Power of Colour Psychology
Before we dive into combinations and schemes, it’s crucial to understand the psychology of colours. Different hues elicit different feelings and emotions. For instance, blue often signifies tranquillity and calmness, while red embodies passion and intensity. Green is synonymous with nature and rejuvenation, while yellow is frequently associated with happiness and creativity. Using these psychological triggers, you can create spaces that embody the feelings you wish to evoke.
A Rainbow of Possibilities: Colour Schemes
When it comes to harmonising hues, understanding various colour schemes is pivotal. These are some of the most commonly used schemes in interior design:
Monochromatic: A monochromatic scheme uses different shades, tints, or tones of a single colour. It creates a clean, elegant, and cohesive look.
Analogous: This scheme uses colours that are next to each other on the colour wheel. It provides more variety while maintaining harmony and unity.
Complementary: Complementary colours are opposites on the colour wheel. When used together, they produce a vibrant and exciting effect, making each colour stand out.
Triadic: A triadic colour scheme uses colours evenly spaced around the colour wheel. It’s a balanced scheme but more vibrant than analogous ones.
Split-Complementary: This scheme offers the contrast of complementary colours but without the intensity. It involves a base colour and the two colours adjacent to its complementary colour.
Striking the Perfect Balance
Harmonising hues is all about striking the perfect balance. It is essential to consider the 60-30-10 rule, a timeless decorating rule that maintains colour balance. It suggests that 60% of the room should be a dominant colour, 30% a secondary colour, and 10% an accent colour. This rule helps in maintaining proportion and creating a layered, visually appealing effect.
Infusing Your Home with Personality and Energy
The real power of colours shines through when you use them to infuse your home with your unique personality and energy. Consider the function of each room and the mood you want to set. Do you want your living room to be invigorating or calming? Should your bedroom inspire romance or tranquillity? Let these questions guide your colour choices.
Letting Colours Transform Your Space
Now that you’re armed with the knowledge of colour psychology, different colour schemes, and balancing rules, it’s time to let colours transform your space. Remember, the colours in your home should resonate with you. They should speak to your soul, reflect your personality, and bring you joy.
Don’t be afraid to experiment. Start small, perhaps with a single wall or a piece of furniture, and see how the new colour impacts your space and mood. You might be surprised at the transformative power that harmonising hues can have on your home.
Colours are the silent language of your home interiors. They can whisper tranquillity, shout vibrancy, and sing harmony. By unlocking the power of colour combinations, you can infuse your living spaces with style, personality, and positive energy. Unleash the power of colours, and you will indeed unlock the true potential of your living space.

The Importance of Natural Elements

Bringing the Outdoors In
Houseplants

Earthy Materials

For instance, a reclaimed wood table not only creates a stunning focal point but also contributes to sustainability efforts. When choosing materials, look for those with low processing levels to preserve their natural beauty and energy, improving both aesthetics and air quality.
Maximizing Natural Light
Windows and Light Flow

Incorporating Nature-Inspired Décor
Artwork and textiles
Nature-themed décor can significantly elevate your space. Select wall art that showcases calming landscapes, botanical prints, or serene seascapes. Such visuals can transport viewers mentally to a peaceful environment, promoting relaxation. 
In addition, consider textiles with nature-inspired patterns. Floral designs or earth-toned fabrics in cushions, throws, and curtains can add a sense of warmth and comfort. Think of layering soft materials to create depth, which can also be a direct connection to the natural world.
Water Features
To take your tranquil haven to the next level, consider adding water features. The gentle sound of bubbling water from a small indoor fountain can mask distracting noises and enhance your space’s serenity. Research from the Journal of Environmental Psychology shows that the presence of water in environments can decrease stress levels by 25%.
These captivating elements not only provide visual interest but also engage multiple senses. Even a small tabletop fountain can serve as a peaceful focal point, creating a soothing ambiance. While these features require maintenance, the peace they bring is often worthwhile.
Embracing Nature in Your Space
Integrating natural elements into your interior design can significantly enhance your living environment, turning it into a calming haven that nurtures peace and relaxation. With thoughtful inclusion of houseplants, natural materials, abundant light, and nature-themed décor, you create more than just a visually appealing space; you cultivate a lifestyle that prioritizes well-being.

Splash Into Style: A Comprehensive Guide to Monsoon-Ready Home Interiors

As we inch closer to the delightful season of monsoons, the air buzzes with the melody of falling rain, the enchanting aroma of the wet earth, and the heart-warming sight of lush greenery. However, with all its charm, the monsoon also carries a potential menace to your chic home interiors. Not to worry, though! Our below tips will help you ride the tide in style!
Home Health Check-Up!
Before we leap into the puddles, let’s first assess the land. Look around your home for any existing issues or vulnerabilities that might be aggravated during monsoons. Check your walls for peeling paint or wallpaper, examine your wooden furniture for any signs of rot, and make sure all your electrical fittings are in good condition. You wouldn’t go out in the rain without an umbrella, right? Well, your home needs similar prep!
Rain-Proof and Ready!
Next, it’s time to arm your home against the uninvited water guests. Waterproof your walls, ceilings, and floors to keep dampness at bay. Installing door and window seals can be a great defence against water seepage. Give special attention to your wooden furniture by applying a layer of protective sealant. Remember, a little precaution can save you from dealing with warped, stained, or damaged furniture later.
Wave Goodbye to Humidity!
Don’t let the dampness dampen your spirits. We’ve got the perfect strategies to maintain a balanced, comfy atmosphere at home. Proper ventilation is a natural and effective way to control indoor humidity. If your home still feels too damp, consider investing in a dehumidifier. It’s essential to prevent mold and mildew growth, as they love the increased moisture levels. Opt for moisture-resistant materials such as HDF-HMR and finishes such as varnish or lacquer, especially in high-moisture areas like the kitchen and bathroom.
Lighting the Lantern: Bright Ideas for Monsoon Evenings
Lighting can completely alter the mood of your space, especially during gloomy monsoon days. Incorporate adequate lighting that illuminates and enhances your home’s ambience. Go for dimmable lights, quirky lamps, or string lights for an enchanted monsoon feel. And let’s not forget to utilize natural light whenever the sun peeks out!
Protecting Your Furniture & Upholstery!
Furniture and upholstery need special attention during the rainy season. Prevent moisture damage by using furniture covers and fast-drying upholstery fabrics. Consider moisture-resistant and easy-to-clean materials to make your life easier. Regular cleaning and maintenance can increase the longevity of your furniture and keep it looking fresh, even during the monsoons.
Monsoon Makeover: Don’t Forget the Cosiness
Now, let’s make some room for fun! Monsoons are all about snuggling up with a good book and a cup of hot cocoa. Quilts, throws, and rugs can add layers of warmth and cosiness to your space. Bring in elements of nature with indoor plants like ferns and snake plants that love the monsoons. Fight the grey skies outside by adding pops of vibrant colours inside your home. Monsoon-themed accessories like a raindrop-shaped mirror or cloud-shaped cushions could add a delightful charm to your interiors.
Safety First, Always!
Last but not least, monsoons call for enhanced safety measures, especially when it comes to electricity. Ensure all your electrical appliances are properly grounded and protected from potential power surges. Protect your electronic devices from fluctuations with the help of surge protectors. Remember, safety should always be a priority.

The Ultimate Guide to Enamel, Deco, and PU Finishes for Stylish Homes
General • 4 min read
In the world of interior design, paints play a very important or a pivotal role & selecting the perfect paint and polish finish can make all the difference in achieving your desired aesthetic and functional goals. 
In this comprehensive guide, we’ll help you understand these distinct finishes, shedding light on their composition, pros, cons, and the ideal spaces where their transformative powers shine.
Aesthetic Appeal
: Paints come in a variety of colors and finishes, allowing you to customize the look of your space to match your style and preferences. From matte to high-gloss finishes, each type can create a unique visual effect that enhances the overall design of a room.
Durability and Protection
: High-quality paints provide a protective layer that can withstand daily wear and tear. For cabinets, especially in kitchens and bathrooms, durable paints are essential to resist moisture, grease, and stains. On walls, paints protect the surface from scratches, marks, and environmental factors.
Maintenance
: Different paints offer varying levels of ease when it comes to cleaning and maintenance. For instance, semi-gloss and satin finishes are easier to wipe clean and are more resistant to moisture, making them ideal for high-traffic areas like kitchens and bathrooms.
Health and Safety
: Some paints are formulated to be low in volatile organic compounds (VOCs), making them safer for indoor use and better for air quality. Choosing low-VOC paints is important for creating a healthier living environment.
Functionality
: Specialized paints, such as mildew-resistant or fire-retardant paints, serve specific purposes beyond aesthetics. These type’s of paints provide added safety and longevity to the surfaces they cover, ensuring that the cabinets and walls are not just beautiful but also functional and safe.
Enamel Finish: Timeless Durability Meets Design
Enamel finish, a classic choice in interior design, is known for its durability and elegant appearance. This versatile finish is typically made of a base of resins and pigments, which, when cured, result in a hard and smooth surface.
Pros:
Cost effective painting solution
Easy application: Anyone can paint an enamel surface with just a suitable paint brush
Vibrant Colours: Enamel allows for vibrant, long-lasting color’s, perfect for decorative elements.
Cons:
Durability: Enamel is general-purpose option with lower durability.
High instances of re-work due to low reliability.
The curing process of oil based enamel paint can produce a strong odor, requiring proper ventilation during application.
Ideal Applications:
Trim and Moldings: Enamel’s smooth finish highlights architectural details.
Deco Finish: 
Deco paint is a premium paint that enhance the look of surfaces with color, texture, and depth. It’s made of one component, a nitrocellulose lacquer (NC Lacquer)
Pros:
Aesthetic Appeal: Deco finishes offer a unique, textured look that adds character and style.
Color retention: Deco paint has a high color retention capacity
Durable: Deco paint is a high-quality, durable water resistant paint
Cons:
Application Complexity: For achieving a uniform look, it requires professional expertise & equipment (spray gun & compressor)
Safety: Solvent based Paint have higher VOC than water based paints
Cost: Costlier than Enamel paint at Rs.350 to 450 per litre
Ideal Applications:
Accent Walls & Panels: 
Kitchen & Bedrooms shutters: 
PU (Polyurethane) Finish: 
PU is a Luxury product which is hard, stretchable polymer pigment coating commonly used on wooden/metallic surfaces, provides a matte or glossy shine while offering protection against wear and tear. 
PU finish has 2 components, a paint component comprising a hydroxyl-containing film-forming resin and an auxiliary component comprising a curing agent
Pros:
High Durability: Offers superior durability and scratch resistance for high-traffic areas
Higher resistance: Can better withstand moisture and harsh conditions.
Safety: Lower or No VOC 
Cons:
Drying: Takes very long to dry
Application: Ideally requires professional expertise & equipment (spray gun & compressor)
Cost: Costlier than Deco paint at Rs.450 to 600 per litre
Ideal Applications:
Wooden Floors: PU finishes add shine and protect against scratches and spills.
Wooden Furniture: They enhance the beauty and longevity of wooden pieces.
Wooden Doors and Windows: PU finishes provide a polished look and protect against the elements.
Care to be taken while choosing or working with paints
Consider Water-Based Paints
: Water-based paints (Latex or Acrylic) generally have lower VOC levels compared to oil-based paints. They also have quicker drying times and are easier to clean up.
Check the Label
: Look for paint labels that specify low-VOC (Volatile Organic Compounds) or zero-VOC content. This information is usually indicated on the packaging.
Ventilation
: Ensure the area being painted is well-ventilated during and after application to help dissipate any VOCs released into the air.
Elevate Your Space with the Right Finish
Each finish has its unique characteristics, offering a wide spectrum of design possibilities. Whether you opt for the timeless durability of enamel, the textured elegance of Deco, or the gleaming resilience of PU, your choice plays a pivotal role in shaping your living space.
At TheHouseKraft, we understand the art and science behind finishes, and we’re here to guide you in making informed decisions that align with your lifestyle and aspirations. So, embark on your design journey with confidence, and let the magic of finishes transform your living space into a masterpiece. Your home is your canvas; it’s time to paint your dreams with brilliance.

Hardware Care & Precautions:
Precautions while using cleaning agents
 - Rust on channels, hinges, and other metallic parts due to household chemicals, sprays, moisture, or leakage of water. Oil spills are also known to Induce rusting. Care must be taken when using such products around hardware and accessories.
Using the Right Cleaning Tools
 - If you have cabinets that are finished with laminate, acrylic or PU, they need just a quick wipe down with a Micro fibre or just a soft cloth and a mild all-purpose cleaner. One can make a household cleaning solution with a 50:50 mix of white vinegar and water. By doing this regularly, we can avoid oil and grime build-up. Keep an eye on cabinets close to the cooking area, as that's where most cooking oil will build up on cabinet shutters happen. Avoid cleaning agents on Pigmented & Powder coated surfaces, as it can decolour the material.
Stains can be tricky
 - If your cabinets are of finished in wood and polished with melamine, they can be cleaned in pretty much the same way. Wiping any spills at once, saves the surface from staining. Even water can leave a stain, so one needs to be careful!
Hot Vessels or heat exposure
 - Avoid keeping anything that's very hot on wooden or laminate surfaces. Heat leads to cracks on laminate surface & crystalizes the adhesive holding the laminate, which leads to laminate warping & peeling
Observe Leakage's 
- Water leaks from the Hose pipe, kitchen sink or washbasin should be fixed immediately. Moisture over a course of time will ruin carcass and shutters and can cause them to warp. Continuously damp surfaces can lead to mold or fungus creation Inside ply veneers
Counter-Top care 
- Quartz/Marble are porous material's so make sure that you do not let any citrus juices like lime or spices like turmeric water/paste is not spilt on the countertop. Since counter-top materials like quartz are porous, stains may be permanent. So, if there are any spills ensure that they are wiped immediately.

Summary: This section provides comprehensive FAQs about HouseKraft's warranty for modular kitchens and interior work, detailing coverage duration, exclusions like water damage or wear and tear, conditions that void the warranty, and the formal claim process.
FAQs
How long is the warranty on my modular kitchen / interior work?
The warranty period begins from the earlier of two dates: your Deep Cleaning completion or your possession date. In the first year, product, labour, and visitation are all covered. From the second year onwards, the product remains under warranty, but labour and transport are charged separately. Customers looking for extended coverage can opt for the Lifetime Warranty (LTW) upgrade, which must be purchased before project execution begins.


Does the interior warranty cover water damage?
No. Damage caused by water leakage or seepage, drilling over tiled surfaces, or continuous dampness in the building structure is not covered under warranty. TheHouseKraft's warranty applies to manufacturing defects in the installed products; structural or plumbing-related damage falls outside its scope.


What if my wardrobe or kitchen cabinet colour fades over time?
Colour, grain, or texture changes in natural wood, laminates, or similar materials due to exposure to light, sunlight, heat, or moisture are not covered under warranty. These are considered natural material characteristics, not manufacturing defects. The warranty does not guarantee an exact aesthetic or material match over time.


Will my warranty be honoured if I sell my house or the flat changes hands?
No. TheHouseKraft's warranty is non-transferable. Once the covered property or products are resold, whether as part of a property sale or separately, the warranty becomes void for the new owner.


What voids a home interior warranty?
Several situations can void your warranty, including the following:

Misuse, negligence, or accidental damage.
Using inappropriate cleaning agents, chemicals, or abrasives on surfaces.
Altering or modifying products from their originally designed use.
Excessive or commercial use of the space.
Relocating or sanitising the home and its products.
Damage from force majeure events such as floods, earthquakes, or lightning.
Exposure to air pollution, mould, mildew, solvents, or staining from foreign substances.

Are scratches and dents covered under warranty?
No. Scratches, dents, cuts, and similar damage from normal day-to-day use or accidental handling are classified as wear and tear and are not covered under TheHouseKraft's warranty. Coverage applies strictly to manufacturing defects.


What if the product or part I need is no longer available or discontinued?
Replacements are subject to material availability, natural ageing of installed components, and manufacturer discontinuation. While TheHouseKraft will make every effort to source a matching replacement, an exact visual or material match cannot always be guaranteed, especially for products installed several years prior.


Does the warranty cover my balcony furniture or outdoor fittings?
No. Products installed in open areas such as balconies, pre-foyers, and open utility spaces are excluded from warranty coverage, as they are exposed to the elements and conditions beyond controlled indoor environments.


If something can't be repaired, will I get a full replacement?
TheHouseKraft follows a Repair First policy. A replacement is considered only if a repair is deemed unfeasible, and it is provided at TheHouseKraft's discretion. Replacement components carry forward under the original warranty period, and the warranty timeline does not reset. Labour, reinstallation, and any additional carpentry required due to site conditions are chargeable separately.


Does the warranty cover pest damage or mould?
No. Damage resulting from environmental conditions such as mould, mildew, air pollution, or solvent exposure is explicitly excluded. The warranty covers manufacturing defects only; biological or environmental deterioration is outside its scope.


How do I make a warranty claim? Can I call someone directly?
Warranty requests must be submitted formally, either by writing to service.request@thehousekraft.com from your registered email, or through the TheHouseKraft web or mobile app using your registered contact details. Walk-in or informal requests are not the process. Service is available Monday to Saturday, 10am-6pm, excluding national and state holidays.


What if I didn't sign off on the project yet; can I still raise a warranty claim?
No. Warranty claims can only be processed after the full handover is complete and your account has been converted to "Service" status on the TheHouseKraft app. Pending snag items or sign-off delays do not pause the warranty start date, but claims cannot be formally raised until the handover is done.


Does using the wrong cleaning product affect my warranty?
Yes, it does. Using inappropriate cleaning agents, harsh chemicals, detergents, or abrasive methods on wooden surfaces or metallic coatings can damage the finish and cause rusting, and any resulting damage will not be covered under warranty. Always use cleaning methods recommended for your specific materials.
